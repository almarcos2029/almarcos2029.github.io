Pokémon: the best-selling video game franchise

Find out how Pokémon became this popular.

Sept. 20, 2025

Pokémon is a Japanese video game franchise where humans co-exist with creatures known as Pokémon, a large variety of species with unique abilities and special powers. The franchise consists of video games, animated series and films, a trading card game, and other related media. Pokémon is estimated to be the world's highest-grossing media franchise and is one of the best-selling video game franchises.

Pokémon is a role-playing game developed by Game Freak, from an original concept by its founder, Satoshi Tajiri. Released on the Game Boy on February 27, 1996, the games became hits and were followed by manga series, a trading card game, and anime series and films. From 1998 to 2000, Pokémon was exported to the rest of the world, creating an unprecedented global phenomenon dubbed "Pokémania". By 2002, the craze had ended, after which Pokémon became a fixture in pop culture, with new products releasing to this day. In the summer of 2016, the franchise spawned a second craze with the release of Pokémon Go, an augmented reality game developed by Niantic.

Pokémon is jointly owned by three: Nintendo, Game Freak, and Creatures. Game Freak develops the core series RPGs, which are published by Nintendo exclusively for their consoles. Creatures manages the trading card game and the merchandise. The three companies established The Pokémon Company in 1998 to manage the Pokémon property within Asia. The Pokémon anime series and films are co-owned by Shogakukan. Since 2009, The Pokémon Company International, different from The Pokémon Company, has managed the franchise in all regions outside Asia.
